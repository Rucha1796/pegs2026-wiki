## Cross-cutting Themes at PEGS 2026

- **AI has moved from hype to productivity** — Multiple talks emphasized the shift from proof-of-concept to scalable pipelines. The question is no longer *can AI design biologics* but *how fast can we scale it across a portfolio.*
- **Data infrastructure is the bottleneck** — Eli Lilly, Takeda, AstraZeneca all stressed that connected wet/dry lab data ecosystems are prerequisite for AI adoption.
- **Automation enables HT developability** — AstraZeneca's ATLAS system, Merck's robotic workflows, and IPI's antibody platform all highlighted end-to-end automation from sequence to data visualization.
- **Immunogenicity prediction is maturing but imperfect** — T cell epitope prediction is established; B cell epitope prediction is catching up (Discotope 3, RoBep). Clinical data quality remains the limiting factor.
- **De novo design is real but still hard** — RFDiffusion, BindCraft, and CADAbRe showed genuine progress; targeting polar epitopes and achieving high affinity from scratch remain open challenges.
- **Protein language models are powerful but need benchmarking** — NbBench, Ab-VS-Bench, and the immune repertoire classification competition all showed that simple baselines outperform complex models more often than expected.
